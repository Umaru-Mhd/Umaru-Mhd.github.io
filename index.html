Umaru Mohammed

Doctoral Researcher, Future Ecologies Group Lab, School of Design, Southern University of Science and Technology

I am a doctoral researcher working on adaptive, nature-based, and sustainable buildings for healthier and low-carbon environments. My current PhD research explores how biophilic and nature-based strategies can be integrated with adaptive building systems, IAQ/IEQ sensing, CFD airflow analysis, daylight, thermal-comfort evaluation, and building performance simulation.

Research interest

My research sits at the intersection of human-oriented built environments, sustainable building design, and intelligent environmental systems. I focus on indoor air quality, indoor environmental quality, adaptive facades, nature-based design, biophilic design, IoT sensing, net-zero energy buildings, CFD airflow analysis, daylight performance, thermal comfort, and simulation-led building performance evaluation.

The broader goal is to support healthier indoor environments while reducing operational carbon and strengthening the ecological relationship between buildings, occupants, and dense urban contexts.

Current themes

  • Nature-based and biophilic design: vegetation, daylight, material ecology, restorative indoor experience, and future ecological interiors.
  • IAQ and IEQ: ventilation effectiveness, pollutant exposure, thermal comfort, daylight autonomy, occupant perception, and environmental sensing.
  • Adaptive buildings: climate-responsive envelopes, integrated facades, sensing-informed control, and high-performance low-carbon strategies.
  • Simulation and evidence: CFD airflow analysis, building performance simulation, IoT sensing, BIM-supported workflows, and AI-supported design methods.
