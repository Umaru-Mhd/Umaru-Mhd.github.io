Advancing adaptive, nature-based, and sustainable buildings for healthier and low-carbon environments.

I am Umaru Mohammed, a doctoral researcher in the Future Ecologies Group Lab at the School of Design, Southern University of Science and Technology. My work connects biophilic and adaptive design, indoor air quality, building performance, IoT sensing, CFD airflow, thermal comfort, daylight, and intelligent sustainable environments to support healthier low-carbon futures.

Research thesis My PhD research investigates how biophilic and nature-based strategies can be coupled with adaptive building systems, IAQ/IEQ sensing, CFD airflow analysis, daylight, and thermal-comfort evaluation to create healthier low-carbon indoor environments for future high-density cities.

Doctoral Lab

Future Ecologies Group, SUSTech School of Design

My doctoral research is carried out under the Future Ecologies Group Lab at Southern University of Science and Technology. The group develops cross-disciplinary intersections between natural systems and artificial environments for future built environments.

This research context aligns with sustainable and green building design, integrated facades, biodiverse design strategies, AI-supported design, ecological models for artificial environments, and new ways of reconciling nature and high-density cities.

Profile

A research profile connecting human wellbeing, ecology, and building intelligence.

My work sits at the intersection of biophilic design, nature-based design, adaptive buildings, indoor air quality, indoor environmental quality, high-performance envelopes, net-zero energy buildings, daylight autonomy, BIM, CFD airflow analysis, thermal comfort, and sustainable construction practice.

I previously lectured in the Department of Building Engineering at Imam Abdulrahman Bin Faisal University, contributing to teaching and research. My current research direction connects future ecologies, IoT sensing, environmental analytics, building performance simulation, and healthier sustainable built environments.

Doctoral Agenda

Research questions for healthier adaptive buildings

Question 01

How can biophilic and nature-based design improve IEQ?

Exploring vegetation, daylight, material ecology, environmental microbiomes, ventilation, humidity, pollutant exposure, perceived freshness, and restorative spatial experience.

Question 02

How can adaptive buildings respond to air, comfort, and climate?

Studying adaptive envelopes, climate-responsive facades, thermal comfort, IAQ, airflow distribution, daylight, and occupant-centered performance feedback.

Question 03

How can sensing and simulation make design measurable?

Using building performance simulation, CFD airflow analysis, BIM, IoT sensing, environmental data, post-occupancy evidence, and AI-supported design workflows.

Question 04

How can low-carbon systems support future ecological cities?

Connecting nZEB pathways, integrated facades, passive cooling, photovoltaic harvesting, life-cycle assessment, biodiversity, and high-density urban ecological design.
